# Contributing

Thanks for helping improve Awesome OPC.

## What Fits

Good additions should directly help explain, evaluate, or build AI-enabled one-person companies.

Strong fits include:

- company-level benchmarks for real professional work, economic deliverables, business simulations, or long-horizon workplace tasks
- function-specific agentic automation for research, product, software engineering, marketing, sales, CRM, support, finance, operations, legal, or reporting
- GUI, browser, OS, Office, SaaS, command-line, code, data, and tool-use agents that can operate real work systems
- multi-agent workflows that explicitly model roles, SOPs, handoffs, collaboration, or virtual-company organization
- agent governance work on permissions, secrets, audit logs, contextual integrity, prompt injection, tool misuse, and delegated authority
- solopreneur, nanocorp, zero-person company, one-person company, single-shareholder company, and small-business formation resources

## What Does Not Fit

Please do not add:

- generic LLM-agent surveys without a clear company-function or work-automation mapping
- generic prompt-engineering, chatbot, or RAG resources unless they support a concrete OPC workflow
- broad LLM reasoning, latent-space, alignment, or model-architecture papers unless they are used in an agentic work system
- toy multi-agent role-play demos without realistic tasks, tools, verification, or company-operation relevance
- vendor product pages unless they contain durable technical, benchmark, market, or governance evidence

## Entry Format

Use the existing table style:

```markdown
| 2024/12 | [TheAgentCompany](https://arxiv.org/abs/2412.14161) | One concise sentence explaining why it matters for OPC. | [Github](https://github.com/TheAgentCompany) |
```

Use `-` in the `Code` column when no public code, project page, dataset, benchmark, or report page is available.

## Curation Rules

- Prefer primary links: arXiv, publisher pages, official project pages, GitHub repositories, official benchmark pages, official reports, or legal/policy sources.
- Keep descriptions short and specific: explain the company function, workflow, benchmark, or governance problem.
- Separate real OPC/company context from agentic automation research.
- If a paper is only indirectly relevant, explain the bridge explicitly or leave it out.
- Use dates in `YYYY/MM` for papers and `YYYY` for reports or legal resources when no month is clear.
- Avoid duplicate entries unless a resource belongs to a genuinely different layer and the distinction is useful.

## Pull Requests

Please include:

- the category where the item belongs
- why the item is relevant to OPC
- whether code, data, a benchmark, or a project page is available
- whether the item is a paper, benchmark, tool, report, or legal/business context resource

Small focused PRs are easier to review.
